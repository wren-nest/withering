The Bathroom

This room smells something like wheat and, faintly, honey. It reminds you of the hospital.

This room is a bathroom, with cold tiled floors and green paisly wallpaper. Harsh strip lights along the ceiling leave little room for shadows.

You question your thought. This room lacks all furnishings of a bathroom.
How do you know it is one?

The only feature of the space are an array of mirrors. Wide mirrors line every wall, and mirrors of every shape and size are scattered about the room.

They lean against the walls, along the floor. A mirrored box holds a pile of hand mirrors atop it.

After a moment of looking around, you realise why you thought this was a bathroom.
The space is filled with your reflection from every angle, and reflections of those reflections. In some places you can hardly see yourself through the blue-green haze of the mirrors on mirrors on mirrors.

You find two mirrors reflecting into eachother, and try to look at the furthest reflection.
You note how the image gets darker, more distorted with every reflection of itself.

There is something behind you. Looking over your shoulder, the door you came in through is gone.
Now it's just Nothing, a rectangle cut out of reality.

The darkness leaks toward you, creeping like spilt oil across the tiles. You turn to leave.


You can see two ways out. In the far corner of the room is an air vent. The grate over it is loose and it looks like you could fit inside easily. Otherwise there is a panel of mirror with a door handle inset into it. Opening it you see only darkness behind it.



Which way will you go?
