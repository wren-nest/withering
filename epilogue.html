Home.

You are in an empty room.

The only light source is a yellowed lightbulb mounted to the wall. To your left is a battered leather couch covered in a thick layer of dust. You can see the imprint where Someone sat recently.

The only other furniture is a wooden coffee table with elegantly carved legs. Gaps in the dust show where items- perhaps photo frames, or a tea set- once were.

Stained wallpaper peeling off the walls. Floorboards creak underfoot. The air is thick as soup, swimming with grime and rot.

You hear something dripping from the ceiling above you, falling somewhere just ahead of you.
You don't bother looking for the source of the sound.
You know there is only Nothing.
Ahead of you is only darkness.

Even where the flickering light should illuminate, there is only shadow.


Darkness begins to creep into the edges of your vision.
You rub your eyes instinctively, you know it won't go away.


You cannot be here anymore.


There is only empty space before you. You can just barely make out the shape of a window, but that vision fades too.

You can't see your hand before your face anymore, like it isn't there at all.

You can look around, for now.


Where do you look?
