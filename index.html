Home.

You are in an empty room.

The space is lit by a single yellowed lightbulb mounted to the wall. To your left is a battered leather couch covered in a thick layer of dust. You can see the imprint where Someone sat recently.

The only other furniture is a wooden coffee table with elegantly carved legs. Gaps in the dust show where items- perhaps photo frames, or a tea set- once were.

Stained wallpaper is peeling off the walls. Floorboards creak underfoot. The air is thick as soup, swimming with grime and rot.

You hear something dripping from the ceiling above you, falling somewhere just ahead of you.
You try to look for the source of the sound.
But looking up, you see... Nothing.
Ahead of you too, only darkness.

Even where the flickering light should illuminate it, the end of the room before you is shrouded in shadow.


Darkness begins to creep into the edges of your vision.
You rub your eyes, but it doesn't go away.


You know you cannot stay here.


The space before you is shrouded in darkness. You can just barely make out the shape of a doorframe.
Walking through, you can't see your hand before your face, like it isn't there at all.

You sense a wall before you.


Which way will you go?
